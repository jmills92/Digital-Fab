Here is all the documentation for assignment 8!

For assignment 8 - I would like to create a clock using some of the mixed methods that we've learned throughout the quarter. My goal in creating a clock is to have something that's (almost) useful and realistic in a working context. Additionally, it aligns with the theme of many of the projects this quarter - desk objects - like the Vase, Lamp, Scissors and Copper Box Nicknack.

BREAKDOWN OF THE DESIGN

The Base: I plan to use Onshape to create a the circular base and to partially cut in the times in roman numerals.

The hands: I plan on using either onshape or rhino to create the hands of the clock, and then 3D printing them

The connector: I plan on using one of the plastic connectors that Joshua passed out in class to combine the 3 objects together. This will allow them to form as a movable part (although manual)

TIMELINE:

The Base: 30 minutes in Onshape, 30 minutes for laser cutting - on Acrylic

The hands: 1 hour in Onshape, (likely several hours) for printing will give myself two days minimum to plan for printing and any snafus.
